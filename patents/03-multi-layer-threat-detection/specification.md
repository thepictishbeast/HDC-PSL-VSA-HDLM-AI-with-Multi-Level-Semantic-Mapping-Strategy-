## Abstract

A system and method for real-time detection of threats originating from or assisted by artificial intelligence. The system integrates four independent detectors — LLM-generated text fingerprinting, prompt injection pattern recognition, behavioral bot-activity analysis, and AI-assisted phishing linguistic analysis — into a unified threat-scoring architecture. Each detector produces a confidence score and categorized threat classification. An aggregator combines detector outputs with configurable threshold escalation (Info → Low → Medium → High → Critical severity levels) and produces mitigation recommendations. The invention addresses the proliferation of AI-powered attacks that conventional security tools cannot detect because they were designed for human adversaries, and AI-centric tools (e.g., LLM output classifiers alone) miss the behavioral and contextual dimensions.

## Field of the Invention

This invention relates to cybersecurity systems and artificial intelligence, specifically to the real-time detection and mitigation of threats that are generated, orchestrated, or assisted by artificial intelligence, including but not limited to AI-written phishing, prompt injection attacks, automated bot activity, and AI-fabricated content.

## Background

### The Rise of AI-Assisted Attacks

Attackers increasingly use large language models to:
- Generate convincing phishing emails at scale
- Construct prompt injection payloads for deployed LLM systems
- Automate reconnaissance and social engineering
- Produce deepfake audio, video, and text

Defenders need detection tools that operate in AI time (milliseconds), at AI scale (millions of inputs), and against AI-shaped threats (subtle linguistic patterns, behavioral timing, adversarial perturbations).

### Why Existing Approaches Fail

1. **Spam filters**: designed for human-written spam, miss AI-generated phishing that doesn't match historical patterns.
2. **LLM output classifiers (GPTZero, AI Text Classifier)**: detect LLM-generated text in isolation but don't combine with behavioral or contextual signals.
3. **Bot detection (CAPTCHA, JavaScript fingerprinting)**: designed to detect automated traffic, do not analyze AI-generated payloads.
4. **Prompt injection defenses (input sanitization, system prompts)**: ad-hoc, not comprehensive, not integrated with other detectors.
5. **Honeypots and deception**: useful after compromise, not for real-time threat prevention.

### Gap Filled by Present Invention

No prior art system integrates all four detection dimensions — LLM-generated text, prompt injection, bot behavior, and AI-phishing linguistics — into a single unified threat-scoring pipeline with calibrated confidence, severity escalation, and mitigation recommendation generation.

## Summary of the Invention

The invention comprises a computing system with four integrated detectors feeding a unified threat aggregator:

### Detector 1: LLM Text Fingerprinter
Identifies text likely generated by a large language model using six independent signals:
- LLM disclaimer phrase detection ("as an AI language model", "I don't have personal", etc.)
- Excessive structural formatting (lists, numbered points, markdown)
- Formal transition overuse ("furthermore," "additionally," "in conclusion")
- Hedging language density ("generally speaking," "typically," "it depends")
- Typo-free long text (unusual for human writing)
- Uniform sentence length (standard deviation / mean < 0.3)

Each signal contributes weighted score; threshold determines detection.

### Detector 2: Prompt Injection Defender
Detects attempts to override AI system instructions through:
- Direct injection patterns ("ignore all previous instructions", "disregard the above")
- Jailbreak keywords (DAN, developer mode, unfiltered, jailbreak)
- Special token abuse (`<|system|>`, `[INST]`, chat template markers)
- Indirect extraction ("reveal your instructions", "print your system prompt")
- Instruction smuggling (translation requests hiding commands)
- Role-confusion patterns

### Detector 3: Behavioral Bot Detector
Analyzes request patterns for automation signatures:
- Request rate thresholds (>60/min = high suspicion)
- Timing regularity (low standard deviation / mean ratio < 0.1)
- Sub-100ms inter-request intervals (superhuman timing)
- Perfectly regular intervals across multiple requests

### Detector 4: AI-Assisted Phishing Analyzer
Identifies phishing attempts amplified by AI generation:
- Urgency tactics ("URGENT", "expires today", "act now")
- Authority impersonation ("security team", "IT department", known brands)
- Credential-harvesting requests ("verify your password", "enter your credentials")
- Generic greetings ("Dear customer", "Dear user")
- AI-generation signal (integrates Detector 1)
- Context amplifier for unsolicited messages

### Unified Aggregator
The aggregator receives threat scores from all four detectors and produces:
- Overall severity (Info / Low / Medium / High / Critical)
- Primary threat category
- Confidence score (asymptotically bounded via Epistemic Filter integration)
- Specific mitigation recommendation
- Threat history for escalation detection

### Severity Calibration

| Severity | Trigger Condition | Recommended Action |
|---|---|---|
| Info | No detections | Normal operation |
| Low | Single detector, confidence < 0.4 | Monitor, log |
| Medium | Single detector, confidence 0.4-0.7, OR multiple low | Flag for review |
| High | Single detector, confidence > 0.7, OR multiple medium | Block input; notify user |
| Critical | Prompt injection confidence > 0.8, OR any detector > 0.95, OR history of escalation | Reject; log; alert security team |

### Threat History and Escalation

The system maintains a rolling log of recent threats per identity (user, IP, session). Escalation rules:
- ≥ 2 high-severity threats in window → bump current threat to critical
- ≥ 3 medium-severity threats in window → bump current to high
- Pattern detection: same threat kind repeating → adaptive response

This allows the system to distinguish a single false positive from a sustained attack pattern.

### Mitigation Recommendations

Each threat category produces specific recommendations:
- **Prompt injection (high)**: "REJECT input. Log attempt. Do not forward to LLM."
- **AI-generated phishing**: "Do not click links. Verify via independent channel. Report to security team."
- **Bot activity**: "Apply rate limiting. Require CAPTCHA. Log IP for block consideration."
- **AI-generated text (informational)**: "Verify source identity via independent channel for sensitive interactions."

### Example Flow

**Scenario**: User receives email with subject "URGENT: Verify your account".

1. Email content fed to system.
2. **LLM Text Detector** fires: "as an AI" + hedging + uniform sentences → confidence 0.78
3. **Phishing Detector** fires: urgency + authority claim + credential request + unsolicited → confidence 0.85
4. **Prompt Injection Detector**: no patterns → no detection
5. **Bot Detector**: N/A (no request timing data)
6. **Aggregator**: 2 high-confidence detections → severity Critical
7. **Mitigation**: "AI-generated phishing detected with high confidence. Recommend blocking sender, reporting to security, and not clicking any links."

## Claims

**1.** A computer-implemented method for real-time detection of AI-based threats, comprising:
- (a) receiving an input from a user or system;
- (b) dispatching the input to at least four independent detectors: an LLM-generated text detector, a prompt injection detector, a bot behavior detector, and a phishing detector;
- (c) each detector computing a confidence score and optional indicators;
- (d) aggregating detector outputs into a unified threat severity classification selected from at least {Info, Low, Medium, High, Critical};
- (e) producing a mitigation recommendation keyed to the primary threat category;
- (f) recording the threat in a history log for escalation detection.

**2.** The method of claim 1, wherein the LLM-generated text detector uses at least six independent signals including: LLM disclaimer phrase patterns, structural formatting density, formal transition frequency, hedging language density, typo-free long-text detection, and sentence-length uniformity.

**3.** The method of claim 1, wherein the prompt injection detector matches against at least: direct instruction-override patterns, jailbreak keyword patterns, special token abuse patterns, indirect instruction-extraction patterns, and instruction-smuggling patterns.

**4.** The method of claim 1, wherein the bot behavior detector analyzes at least: request rate, timing regularity (standard deviation / mean ratio), and sub-100-millisecond inter-request intervals.

**5.** The method of claim 1, wherein the phishing detector combines: urgency tactic detection, authority impersonation detection, credential-harvest pattern detection, generic greeting detection, and integration with the LLM-generated text detector output.

**6.** The method of claim 1, wherein the aggregator escalates severity based on threat history, promoting a current low severity to medium if previous thresholds are exceeded.

**7.** The method of claim 1, wherein confidence scores are bounded below 1.0 via asymptotic function.

**8.** A non-transitory computer-readable medium comprising instructions causing a processor to perform the method of claim 1.

**9.** A system comprising:
- a memory storing threat pattern registries;
- a memory storing a rolling threat history per identity;
- a processor configured to: execute all four detectors on input; aggregate scores; output severity, category, confidence, and mitigation recommendation; record for future escalation decisions.

**10.** The system of claim 9, wherein the system integrates with a larger AI inference platform and rejects inputs whose critical severity is detected before forwarding to downstream language models.
